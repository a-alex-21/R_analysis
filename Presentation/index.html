Research Question

Has the world become a better place?

02
Instead of relying on one indicator, the analysis compares several dimensions of human wellbeing.
Maslow's hierarchy helps organize indicators from basic survival to freedom and participation.
The comparison is continental: past values closest to 1990 versus present values closest to 2020.

Initial expectation

Progress should be clearest in basic needs such as health, education, and economic conditions, while social belonging and democracy may be more uneven.

Conclusion

The evidence points to a better world today

15
The clearest improvements are in basic and middle needs: people live longer, literacy is higher, and GDP per capita increased across all continents.
Safety and democracy also improved in most regions, even though the pattern is not perfectly universal.
The only strongly ambiguous indicator is one-person households, which reflects social change rather than a simple decline.

Final answer

Yes, by the indicators in this analysis, the world is generally a better place now than it was in the past. Progress is strongest in health, education, economic conditions, and many aspects of freedom, while some social and political differences still remain.
